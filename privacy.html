Juno Privacy Policy

Effective date: 16 April 2026 Last updated: 16 April 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to Juno (the "App"). Juno is an AI-powered personal assistant application for iOS that helps you manage your daily life by connecting to the services you already use. This Privacy Policy explains how Brightboard AI LTD ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, shares, and protects your personal information when you use our App and related services (collectively, the "Services").

By downloading, installing, or using the App, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with the practices described herein, please do not use the Services.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information you provide directly

  • Account information: When you create a Juno account, we collect your name, email address, and authentication credentials.
  • Chat and voice input: Messages, prompts, and voice recordings you submit to the AI assistant, including text typed in the chat interface and audio captured when using voice features.
  • Preferences and settings: Your configuration choices, including voice style, habit routines, reminder settings, and notification preferences.
  • Payment information: If you subscribe to a paid plan (e.g. Plus or Pro), payment details are processed by our third-party payment provider. We do not store full payment card numbers on our servers.

2.2 Information from connected services

Juno's core value is integrating with your existing tools. When you connect a third-party service, we access only the data necessary to provide the requested functionality:

IntegrationData accessed
Email (Gmail, Outlook) Email metadata, subject lines, message bodies, and attachments necessary to display, search, and draft emails on your behalf.
Calendar (Google, Apple, iCal) Calendar events, attendees, locations, and scheduling data to display your agenda and create events.
Reminders / Tasks Task titles, due dates, completion status, and associated notes.
Slack Workspace identity, channel lists, and message content to read, draft, and send Slack messages.
Wallet / Payments Transaction history, payment approval records, and wallet configuration for payment management features.
Uber Ride request data and trip information when you initiate rides through the App.

We access this data via OAuth tokens or secure API connections that you explicitly authorise. You can disconnect any integration at any time through the Tool Manager in the App's Core section.

2.3 Information collected automatically

  • Device and usage data: Device model, operating system version, unique device identifiers, crash logs, and general usage analytics (e.g. features used, session duration).
  • Usage statistics: Aggregated interaction counts displayed in the Usage panel (e.g. weekly interaction totals) are computed locally or server-side to show you your activity.
  • Log data: Server logs that may include your IP address, request timestamps, and API endpoints accessed.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To provide and operate the Services: Processing your chat messages, executing actions across connected apps, delivering morning briefings, running habit routines, and surfacing relevant information from your connected accounts.
  • To personalise your experience: Tailoring AI responses based on your preferences, prior interactions, and connected account data.
  • To improve the Services: Analysing aggregated, de-identified usage patterns to improve features, fix bugs, and develop new functionality.
  • To communicate with you: Sending transactional notifications (e.g. morning briefings, habit reminders, payment approvals) and, where you have opted in, marketing communications.
  • To ensure security and prevent abuse: Detecting and preventing fraud, unauthorised access, and other harmful activities.
  • To comply with legal obligations: Responding to lawful requests from regulatory authorities and law enforcement.

4. AI Processing and Automated Decision-Making

Juno uses artificial intelligence to understand your requests, draft communications, summarise information, and take actions on your behalf. When you ask Juno to perform a task (e.g. draft an email reply, create a calendar event, or send a Slack message), the AI processes the relevant context from your connected accounts to generate a response or action.

  • Human review: We do not review your individual conversations. All conversation data is anonymised and encrypted.
  • Automated actions: Juno may draft or suggest actions (e.g. reply to an email, approve a payment), but actions with significant consequences require your explicit confirmation before execution.

5. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell your personal data. We may share information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Third-party service providers: We share data with vendors who help us operate the Services (e.g. cloud hosting, analytics, payment processing). These providers are contractually required to use your data only on our behalf and in accordance with this policy.
  • Connected integrations: When you instruct Juno to take an action (e.g. send an email, post a Slack message), the relevant data is transmitted to the corresponding third-party service through their API.
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose information if required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request.
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you of any such change.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the Services. Chat history and AI interaction logs are retained for the duration of your account unless you delete them. When you disconnect an integration, we stop accessing new data from that service and delete cached data within 30 days. If you delete your account, we will delete or anonymise your personal data within 90 days, except where retention is required by law.

7. Data Security

We implement industry-standard technical and organisational measures to protect your data, including encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256), access controls and authentication for internal systems, regular security assessments and penetration testing, and secure OAuth token storage for connected integrations. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure. If you become aware of a security vulnerability, please contact us immediately at help@juno-labs.ai.

8. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights:

  • Access and portability: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Correction: Request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Deletion: Request that we delete your personal data, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Restriction and objection: Request that we restrict processing or object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Withdraw consent: Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
  • Disconnect integrations: You can revoke access to any connected service at any time through the Tool Manager in the App.

To exercise these rights, please contact us at help@juno-labs.ai. We will respond within 30 days (or as required by applicable law).

9. International Data Transfers

To provide the Services, your personal data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside the United Kingdom, including the United States, where our AI infrastructure and certain cloud service providers are based.

9.1 UK–US Data Bridge

Where we transfer personal data from the UK to the United States, we rely on the UK–US Data Bridge (the UK Extension to the EU–US Data Privacy Framework) where our US-based service providers are certified under the Data Privacy Framework with the UK Extension. We verify each provider's active certification status on the Data Privacy Framework List (dataprivacyframework.gov) and monitor it on an ongoing basis.

9.2 Alternative Safeguards

Where a US-based provider is not certified under the Data Privacy Framework, or where we transfer data to recipients in other countries outside the UK, we put in place appropriate safeguards as required by UK GDPR, such as the UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or the UK Addendum to EU Standard Contractual Clauses. These safeguards ensure that your personal data receives a level of protection equivalent to that afforded under UK data protection law.

9.3 Your rights regarding international transfers

You have the right to request details of the safeguards we have in place for any specific international transfer of your personal data. To make such a request, please contact us at help@juno-labs.ai.

10. Children's Privacy

The Services are not directed to individuals under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If we become aware that we have collected data from a child under 16, we will take steps to delete it promptly. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us at help@juno-labs.ai.

11. Third-Party Services

The App integrates with third-party services (e.g. Gmail, Slack, Uber). Your use of these services is governed by their own privacy policies and terms of service. We encourage you to review the privacy practices of each service you connect. Juno is not responsible for the data practices of third-party services.

12. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

The App itself does not use browser cookies. We may use mobile analytics SDKs to collect anonymised usage data for performance monitoring and improvement. You can opt out of analytics collection in the App's Settings.

13. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will notify you through the App or by email at least 14 days before the changes take effect. Your continued use of the Services after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
